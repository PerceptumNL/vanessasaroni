# Vanessa Saroni Brandbook

Born in Brazil. Made in Italy. Designed in Amsterdam.


---


## 1. The Brand

Vanessa Saroni makes luxury handbags from handwoven fabric. The fabric is woven by women artisans in Brazil. The bags are assembled by craftspeople in Italy. The designs come from Vanessa's studio on the Prinsengracht in Amsterdam.

No two pieces of fabric are identical. The variations in tension, in colour depth, in the way the weft catches the light: those are not imperfections. They are proof that a person made this, not a machine.

The brand is named after its founder. It sounds like her, it looks like her, and it reflects her life: born in Brazil, fell in love with Italy, now lives in Amsterdam. Every bag carries all three places.

**Tagline:** Born in Brazil. Made in Italy. Designed in Amsterdam.

**In one sentence:** Vanessa Saroni makes luxury handbags from handwoven Brazilian fabric, assembled in Italy, designed in Amsterdam.

## 2. Brand Values

Five values shape every decision. They are listed in order of how they should appear in communication: craftsmanship first, sustainability last.

### Craftsmanship
Each metre of fabric takes a full day to weave by hand on a traditional loom. The bags are assembled in Italy by skilled craftspeople. This is the core of the brand: human hands, real skill, visible quality.

### Women's Empowerment
The brand is made by women, for women. The artisans in Brazil gain economic independence through their craft. The "Mary for Life" initiative supports young mothers in Italy. This is not charity. It is the business model.

### Cultural Heritage
The fabrics carry the textile traditions of Brazilian artisan communities. The construction carries Italian leather craftsmanship. The design carries Dutch directness and clarity. The brand is a cultural mosaic.

### Timelessness
Collections do not follow seasons. They are designed to stay. Handwoven cotton that softens with use, Italian hardware that holds, designs that look as good in five years as they do today.

### Sustainability
Handwoven production uses no industrial electricity, no excess water, no factory waste. But we do not lead with this. We lead with beauty. Sustainability is what happens when you make things by hand, with care. It is a consequence, not a marketing angle.

## 3. Who We Speak To

Our audience is women aged 28 to 55 with an appreciation for craftsmanship and design. They buy fewer, better things. They care about where products come from and who made them. They are drawn to unique pieces that tell a story.

### The Conscious Creative
Works in a creative or professional field. Shops intentionally, researches brands, values transparency. Would rather own 5 meaningful accessories than 50 disposable ones.

*She finds us through: Instagram, sustainable fashion communities, word of mouth.*

### The Cultural Explorer
Loves travel, art, design. Drawn to objects with cultural depth and provenance. Buys from artisans, not airports. Our tricultural origin story is exactly what attracts her.

*She finds us through: design publications, travel content, gallery events.*

### The Quiet Luxury Advocate
Has outgrown logo-driven brands. Prefers understated quality that insiders recognise. Appreciates "Made in Italy" without needing a heritage house label.

*She finds us through: peer recommendations, press coverage, private viewings.*

### The Female Founder
Entrepreneur or leader who actively supports women-led businesses. The empowerment narrative resonates personally. Likely to become a brand ambassador.

*She finds us through: LinkedIn, founder networks, events.*

**What this palette says:** warm, natural, grounded. It references earth tones, raw materials, and sunlight. It is never cold, never neon, never sterile.

### Typography

**Headlines:** Playfair Display (serif)
- Weight: 400 (regular) and 500 (medium)
- Italic for emotional emphasis and quotes
- Used for: page titles, section headings, bag names, blockquotes

**Body and UI:** Inter (sans-serif)
- Weight: 300 (light) for body, 400 (regular) for emphasis, 500 (medium) for labels
- Used for: body text, navigation, buttons, captions, metadata

**The combination says:** classic meets modern. The serif carries warmth and personality. The sans-serif carries clarity and precision. Together they reflect the brand: Brazilian warmth, Italian elegance, Dutch directness.

### Logo

The wordmark is "VANESSA SARONI" in a geometric sans-serif with wide letter-spacing. All capitals.

**Usage rules:**
- Always use the provided wordmark files. Never set it in a different typeface.
- Minimum clear space: the height of the letter "A" on all sides.
- On light backgrounds: use black wordmark.
- On dark backgrounds: use white wordmark.
- Never stretch, rotate, add effects, or place on busy photo backgrounds without adequate contrast.
- The decorative "S" monogram (cursive) may be used as a secondary mark in corners and small applications.

### Photography Direction

**The brand looks like:**
- Natural light. Golden hour. Soft shadows.
- Real settings: cafes, galleries, markets, streetscapes, homes, studios. Not sterile white studios.
- Texture close-ups: hands on fabric, weave patterns, leather grain, thread on a loom.
- Women in real moments, not posed fashion shots. The bag completes the picture; it does not dominate it.
- Multicultural visual language: Brazilian colours, Italian architecture, Dutch interiors. Subtle, not literal.

**The brand does NOT look like:**
- Flash photography or harsh overhead light.
- Pure white e-commerce backgrounds with nothing else.
- Models posed stiffly in a studio.
- Stock photography of any kind.
- Heavy retouching or colour grading that removes natural warmth.
- The bag floating alone on a blank page without context.

**People in photos:**
- Diverse. Multicultural. Reflecting the brand's Brazilian, Italian, and Dutch roots.
- Sophisticated but approachable. Not intimidating.
- Aged 28 to 55 (matching our audience).
- Styled in a way that looks effortless, not over-produced.

## 5. Voice & Tone

The brand voice is the personality. It never changes. The tone adjusts to context.

### Voice: Who We Are

| Trait | What it means | What it sounds like |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Friendly** | We start conversations, not campaigns. We include, not exclude. | "You all know Karol in all her unique sophistication, but today she is showing up in a brand new red tartan version." |
| **Genuine** | We are connected to our roots. We honour heritage. We are honest. | "The imperfections are not flaws. They are proof that a person made this, not a machine." |
| **Witty** | We have humour. We do serious work with grace. | "Class used to be priceless. Now from 675 euro." / "3 bags in 1. What else?" |
| **Passionate** | We love what we do. We celebrate the women who make it possible. | "I want you to choose our bags because they are beautiful, and then discover they were made the right way." |

### Tone: How We Adjust

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Instagram caption | Playful, conversational, personal | "Anytime and anywhere you need her, Juju is here for you because she is the perfect bag for any occasion." |
| Product page | Evocative, warm, slightly poetic | "Lola is easy. She sits on your shoulder like she has always been there." |
| Email newsletter | Intimate, personal, like writing to a friend | "I just got back from the workshop in Italy and I need to tell you what I saw." |
| Press/media | Confident, factual, culturally rich | "Vanessa Saroni combines Brazilian artisanal textile traditions with Italian luxury manufacturing." |
| LinkedIn | Professional but warm, empowerment-focused | "Something truly unique can only be achieved by the work and collaboration of restless individuals." |
| Customer service | Warm, patient, personal | "Thank you for reaching out. I would love to help you find the right bag." |

### Voice Do's and Don'ts

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Be conversational | Be snobby or condescending |
| Share interesting context | Sound like a professor lecturing |
| Be playful | Force jokes or use memes |
| Make unexpected connections to art and design | Use commonplace or cliche references |
| Be honest and direct | Use obscure references your audience might not get |
| Reference Brazilian and Italian culture | Use overplayed examples unrelated to the brand |
| Use strong, confident verbs | Be lukewarm or half-hearted |
| Champion sustainable practices naturally | Be preachy about sustainability |
| Celebrate women's achievements | Be over the top enthusiastic |
| Write in first person (as Vanessa) | Write in third person corporate voice |

## 6. Writing for the Brand

### Product Copy Formula

Each bag has a name, a personality, and a voice. Write about her like she is a person you are introducing to a friend.

**Structure:**
1. **Tagline** (5 to 10 words, witty or evocative)
2. **Introduction** (2 sentences: who she is, what she does)
3. **Fabric story** (2 sentences: how she is made, what makes the material special)

**Example: Juju**
- Tagline: "Class used to be priceless."
- Introduction: "Juju is our signature piece. A top handle bag that walks into a room before you do."
- Fabric: "Hand-woven tartan in forest green and warm grey. Each Juju takes three days of weaving before she even reaches the Italian workshop."

**Example: Karol**
- Tagline: "3 bags in 1. What else?"
- Introduction: "Karol is clever. A belt bag, a clutch, and a crossbody in one design."
- Fabric: "Piano weave with Italian leather detailing. The combination of handwoven cotton and structured leather gives Karol her distinctive character."

**Example: Mary**
- Tagline: "Our smallest bag with our biggest purpose."
- Introduction: "Mary is special. A phone and wrist pouch whose revenue goes directly to Mary for Life."
- Fabric: "Available in Piano Grey, Mustard, Off-White, and Allegro Black. Each Mary is woven with the same care as our largest bags."

### Copywriting Rules

1. **Give each bag a personality.** She is not an "item" or a "product". She has a name, a character, a way of being.
2. **Lead with emotion, follow with craft.** First tell someone why they would love this bag. Then tell them how it was made.
3. **Be specific.** "Each metre takes a full day to produce" is better than "our artisans spend time on each piece."
4. **Use second person.** "When you hold one of our bags" is more intimate than "the customer will experience."
5. **Avoid feature lists.** Do not list "100% cotton, handwoven, Italian leather, gold hardware". Weave the details into narrative.
6. **Use real numbers.** "1 day per metre." "Height 30 cm, length 27 cm, depth 16 cm." Specifics build trust.

### Words We Use

| Instead of | We say |
|-----------|--------|
| Product | Piece, bag, her (by name) |
| Customer | You, her, the women who carry our bags |
| Purchase / Buy | Choose, find, bring home |
| Affordable | Accessible (if comparison is ever needed) |
| Eco-friendly | Handwoven, by hand, without factories |
| High quality | Crafted, hand-finished, built to last |
| Collection launch | New chapter, new story |

### Words We Never Use

Innovative. Unique (overused). State-of-the-art. Cutting-edge. Next-level. Disruptive. Solutions. Synergy. Leverage. Cheap. Affordable. Budget-friendly. Sale. Discount. Limited-time offer. Unboxing. Haul.

| Format | Frequency | Best for |
|--------|-----------|---------|

| Carousels | 1 to 2/week | A fabric's journey, a design's story, a customer spotlight, a "why this price" explainer |

| Stories | Daily | Real-time: packing orders, studio life, travel, event prep, polls, Q&As |

### Channel-Specific Guidance

**Instagram** (primary channel)
- Tone: playful, personal, conversational
- Content: product stories, behind-the-scenes, customer features, design inspiration
- Prefer: educating about the product over generic captions
- Captions: tell a story, ask a question, make the reader feel included
- "More" example: "Anytime and anywhere you need her, Juju is here for you because she is the perfect bag for any occasion. Discover her in her new black and white look."
- "Less" example: "Juju is the perfect bag for you."

**LinkedIn** (secondary, brand building)
- Tone: professional but warm, empowerment-focused, culturally rich
- Content: product news, recruiting content, media mentions, "Our Women" stories
- Focus: the people, the story, the craft, not the product

**Facebook** (secondary, mirrors Instagram)
- Tone: same as Instagram but slightly longer-form
- Content: shares Instagram posts, event announcements, community stories

### Social Media Do's and Don'ts

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Explore emotional approaches easy to connect with | Write generic captions that could be any brand |
| Prefer talking about intangible aspects and benefits | List functional specifications |
| Educate the audience about the product and craft | Post product-only shots with no story |
| Use references to art and design as relevant | Use slangs or memes |
| Respond to every comment and DM within 4 hours | Ignore community engagement |

## 8. The Rules

These are non-negotiable. They apply to everyone who creates anything for the brand.

1. **Never discount.** Never negotiate on price. Never run a "sale." If you want to add value, gift an accessory or offer exclusive access to the next collection. Price firmness is a quality signal.

2. **Never use "cheap", "affordable", or "budget-friendly."** The word is "accessible" if a comparison is ever needed. But even that should be rare.

3. **Never use stock photography.** All imagery must be authentic to the brand. If you do not have the right image, do not publish. Wait.

4. **Never position sustainability as the headline.** It is a supporting value. Lead with beauty, craftsmanship, and the women who make it. Sustainability is what happens naturally when you make things by hand.

5. **Every communication should feel like it comes from Vanessa.** Not from "the brand", not from "the team", not from "management." From a person. First person preferred.

6. **Never describe the bags in purely functional terms.** "Dimensions: 30 x 27 x 16 cm" is fine in a spec line. But the copy should sell the feeling, the story, the experience.

7. **Never publish content that could be any brand.** If you remove the name "Vanessa Saroni" from a piece of content and it could belong to any other accessories brand, rewrite it.

8. **Bags are referred to by name and treated as characters.** She is Juju, not "the top handle bag in tartan." She has a personality, a story, a way of being.

9. **No seasonal urgency or manufactured FOMO.** The bags are limited by nature (250 total). The scarcity is real. State it factually: "Only 7 remaining." Never: "HURRY! Almost sold out!!"

10. **Respect the cultural origins.** Brazilian culture, Italian craftsmanship, and Dutch design are referenced with knowledge and specificity. Never superficially, never as decoration.
